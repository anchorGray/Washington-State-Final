Welcome to Tacoma

"T-Town"

Tacoma is a mid-sized urban port city and the county seat of Pierce County of Washington State. Tacoma is the second-largest city in the Puget Sound area and the third-largest in the state with a population of 221,776. The median income for the city is 69,956 (USC, 2023). Tacoma is home to the University of Washington Tacoma, Pacific Lutheran University, and the University of Puget Sound.

Tacoma is home to a number of museums including the Washington State History Museum, the Tacoma Art Museum, the Museum of Glass, the America's Car Museum, and the LeMay Car Museum (PCW, 2023). In addition to Tacoma's museums, the city is proximal to Mt Rainier National Park. Mt Rainier is the highest mountain in Washington State and the Cascade Range. The surrounding park is 369 square miles and is home to 26 major glaciers. It is an active volcano and is the most glaciated peak in the contiguous U.S.A., spawning six major rivers. The park contains outstanding examples of old-growth forests and subalpine meadows. Mt Rainier is a popular destination for hiking, climbing, and scenic drives (NPS, 2023).

Washington State is the premiere locale for all things arts, culture, activities, and entertainment!
